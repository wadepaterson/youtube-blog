5 Tips for Choosing the Perfect Toastmasters Word of the Day

By Wade Paterson

Idiosyncratic. Serendipitous. Nostalgia. How do you choose the right word for the Word of the Day when you're serving as the Toastmasters Grammarian? Expanding our vocabularies and using powerful words is a vital skill for speakers — it allows us to connect more deeply with our audiences. Whether you're a seasoned Grammarian or stepping into the role for the first time, these five tips will help you select the perfect word for your next meeting.

1. Use AI Tools to Match the Meeting Theme

A great Word of the Day is usually tied to the theme of the meeting, and AI tools like ChatGPT can make finding that connection effortless. Simply type in a prompt such as, "Can you give me 30 words related to the Toastmasters theme of board games? These words are for the Grammarian role — I'm trying to come up with a Word of the Day." In seconds, you'll have a curated list of options to choose from.

If you're not a fan of AI tools, you can always go old school and use a thesaurus. Start with keywords related to the theme, then explore synonyms and related terms until you find something a little less common — something worthy of introducing to your club.

Whatever method you use, remember: a great Word of the Day should be challenging enough that we don't use it in everyday conversation, but not so obscure that nobody would ever realistically use it.

2. Listen Carefully in Everyday Conversations

Some of the best words come from the world around you. Pay close attention during your daily conversations, podcasts, meetings, or reading. When you hear a word that catches your attention because it's uncommon or striking, start a running note list in your phone and jot it down — even if you don't know the exact meaning at the time.

This simple habit gives you a growing bank of words to draw from. And here's the beauty of it: if a word caused confusion or intrigue for you, it's almost guaranteed to spark interest for your fellow club members as well.

3. Use Merriam-Webster's Word of the Day

Why reinvent the wheel? Merriam-Webster already offers a fantastic Word of the Day feature on their website. Each day, a new word is highlighted with its definition, pronunciation, and usage examples. Even better, you can browse previous days' selections, which gives you flexibility to find a word that aligns with your meeting's theme.

This is an incredibly convenient resource, especially when you're short on time and need reliable inspiration.

4. Introduce a Newly Added Word

Rizz. Greedflation. New words are constantly being added to the dictionary every year, and introducing one of these fresh additions can make your Word of the Day feel modern and relevant. Consider these benefits:

  • You'll keep your club current with evolving language trends.
  • You'll help members stay aware of new lingo being used in everyday culture.
  • You'll add a creative and fun element to the meeting that sparks conversation.

Bringing a brand-new word to the table is a fantastic way to keep the Grammarian role fresh and engaging.

5. Choose a Commonly Misused Word

This final tip doubles as a powerful educational moment for your club. There are plenty of words that people use incorrectly without even realising it. Think of classic examples like:

  • Accept (to receive) vs. Except (to exclude)
  • Principle (a fundamental truth) vs. Principal (a leader or primary thing)

These words sound similar but carry completely different meanings. By selecting one and clearly explaining the distinction, you turn the Word of the Day into a genuine teaching moment. This is especially impactful if you've noticed club members using certain words incorrectly in the past — it's a tactful way to help everyone sharpen their vocabulary.

Conclusion

Choosing a Word of the Day doesn't have to be stressful. Whether you lean on AI tools, keep a running list from real-life conversations, browse Merriam-Webster, highlight newly coined terms, or spotlight commonly misused words, you now have a toolkit of strategies to make your Grammarian role shine. The key is to select a word that is interesting, accessible, and — whenever possible — connected to the theme of your meeting. With a little preparation, you'll elevate both your own skills and the vocabulary of your entire Toastmasters club.
